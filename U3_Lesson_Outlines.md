## HOW THIS UNIT WORKS — THE GO VIRAL CHALLENGE

U3 is delivered entirely through the **Go Viral Challenge**. Learners are given a brief: build a social media brand in their chosen Creative Industries sector and grow an audience. They work through the full creative process — research, development, review — in a live, real-stakes context rather than a theoretical one.

This is explicitly permitted and encouraged by SQA. The NAB notes state the unit is ideally suited to "a website or blog", "an electronic press kit" and similar digital contexts, and that centres may develop their own brief. A social media brand campaign is a legitimate and powerful context for all three outcomes.

**The brief is the anchor.** Every outcome maps to the challenge:
- **O1 Research stage** — analyse the brief, research the landscape, produce a personal brand concept, present it, agree team direction
- **O2 Development stage** — build the brand, create content, implement roles, develop and refine over multiple weeks
- **O3 Evaluation** — summative evaluation of the whole process at the end of term

**The Friday double period rhythm:**
- **Fri P1 (50 min)** — Active making. Content creation, brand building, platform work, audience engagement. This is the doing.
- **Fri P2 (50 min)** — Short weekly review. What did I post, what happened to the numbers, what am I trying next week. Formative and habitual — not laboured.

**The weekly Friday review is NOT the O3 evidence.** It feeds O3 but the formal summative evaluation happens in week 17. The weekly reviews are the development log — a running record of the creative process in action.

**Observation checklists are required for O1 PCs (b)(c)(d)(e) and O2 all PCs.** These must be completed during the team presentation sessions and the weekly making sessions. Flag them clearly throughout these outlines.

## THE GO VIRAL BRIEF
*(Issued to learners in week 9 as a preview, formally launched week 10)*

**Client:** Your Creative Industries sector audience

**Background:** The Creative Industries are built on visibility. Practitioners who succeed don't just have talent — they know how to build an audience, communicate their work and establish a presence. Your challenge is to do exactly that.

**Requirements:** You are invited to develop a social media brand in your chosen Creative Industries sector. Your brand should: have a clear identity and target audience; produce regular original content relevant to your sector; grow a following over the course of the challenge; and demonstrate a clear, documented creative process from initial research through to evaluation.

**Considerations/constraints:**
- Platform choice must be appropriate for your sector and target audience
- Content must be original and sector-relevant
- Budget: zero — creativity over cash
- Health, safety and safeguarding: all content must be appropriate, no personal identifying information shared publicly, school social media guidelines apply
- Timeline: 7 weeks of active content creation (wks 11–17), with research in wk 10 and evaluation in wk 17

**Client expectations:** A creative, achievable brand concept presented to the team; evidence of a sustained development process; a final summative evaluation of whether the brief was met.

**Delivering the response:** Personal concept presented to the team in week 10. Team agrees a shared direction. Each learner then develops their own individual brand within the agreed team framework.

### Wed 22 Oct — Session 22: Personal Brand Concept — Full Response (O1 PC a)
**Duration:** 100 minutes
**Learning intention:** Produce a complete personal response to the brief — the brand concept document.

| Time | Activity |
|------|----------|
| 0–15 | **Brief recap + model response.** Show a model personal response (teacher-created example for Go Viral context). Walk through what each section needs — not just what the idea is but how it looks/sounds, who it's for, what it costs, timescale, H&S/safeguarding, skills/resources/equipment needed, and how it meets the brief. Set the quality bar. |
| 15–75 | **Personal brand concept document (NAB O1 PC a).** Learners produce their full personal response covering: (1) how the brand would look and sound — name, visual identity, tone of voice, content style, (2) target market — specific and evidenced from research, (3) cost considerations — what is free, what might cost anything, (4) timescale — posting schedule, when to expect growth, (5) H&S and safeguarding — what content guidelines apply, what won't be shared publicly, (6) skills/resources/techniques/equipment — what they already have, what they need to learn, (7) how the response meets the requirements of the brief. Teacher circulates and challenges surface-level thinking — "Why that platform? Why that content style? Show me the evidence from your research." |
| 75–90 | **Pre-presentation teacher feedback.** Learners share their concept with the teacher before the team presentation. Teacher gives brief verbal feedback: is it creative but manageable? Does it address all the brief requirements? Learner notes any changes. This is specifically recommended in the SQA guidance. |
| 90–100 | **Presentation preparation.** Learners decide how they will present their concept to the team on Friday — verbally with notes, a short slide, a visual mock-up? Brief plan made. |

| Time | Activity |
|------|----------|
| 0–10 | **Briefing: How to give and receive feedback.** Quick input on constructive feedback — specific, evidence-based, not just "I liked it". ## WEEKS 12–16 — 3 Nov to 5 Dec 2025
## THE DEVELOPMENT STAGE — Heart of the Unit

The following five weeks follow the same weekly rhythm. Each week has:
- **Tue double (100 min)** — Development support session: themed input, peer critique, group problem-solving, applying new techniques
- **Wed double (100 min)** — Content creation workshop: making time, applying the week's theme
- **Fri P1 (50 min)** — Go Viral making: create, post, engage
- **Fri P2 (50 min)** — Weekly review: data, reflection, intention

The Tuesday and Wednesday sessions are not filler — they are where the craft develops and where team collaboration is most visible. Each week has a different focus to develop learners' understanding of content strategy progressively.
