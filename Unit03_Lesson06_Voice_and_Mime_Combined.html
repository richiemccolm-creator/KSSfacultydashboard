WARM UP·Voice and body — at the same time0–10 min
0–10
minutes
Combined Warm-Up
1
Walk the space as a character — any character. Your teacher calls a vocal change (fast pace, low pitch, formal register) without stopping the movement.
2
Your body must keep applying S.P.E.C.S. while your voice adapts. The two must never cancel each other out.
3
Notice: when your voice changes, does your body instinctively want to change too? Let it. Voice and body communicate the same character — they should agree.
4
Freeze. Look around the room. Can you read everyone else's character from their body and voice together — even without hearing their words clearly?
DISCUSS
When voice and body communicate the same character — what makes the performance more powerful than using either one alone?

Combined Performance
SCENARIO — SAME FOR EVERYONE
Two people. One of them has something to tell the other that will change everything. The listener already suspects what it is. Neither of them says what it actually is — directly.
PLAN YOUR S.P.E.C.S.
Which S.P.E.C.S. elements define your character's physical life?
Where in the scene does your body change — and why?
What is your character's most important single physical moment?
PLAN YOUR P.P.V.P.I.R.
What register does your character use — and does it shift under pressure?
Where does your pitch drop or rise — and what triggers it?
What is your character's most important single vocal moment?
1
Plan for 5 minutes. Rehearse for 10. Then perform — no notes, no stopping, no breaking character.
2
Audience evaluates using both S.P.E.C.S. and P.P.V.P.I.R. simultaneously.
