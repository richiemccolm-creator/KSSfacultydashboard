Vocabulary Focus  ·  New Today

Stance and Mannerisms

Use both words in your written reflection and exit ticket today.

ST
Stance
How someone stands on stage — width of feet, direction of body, weight distribution — adjusted deliberately to show character, status or emotion. Every position is a choice.
e.g. "The villain entered with a wide, grounded stance — both feet planted, chest open, chin raised. He owned the stage."
In life: how you stand when you enter a room, sit in a meeting or wait at the front of a class communicates everything before you open your mouth
MN
Mannerisms
A character's personal habits and distinctive physical behaviours — the small, repeated movements that reveal personality or emotional state without the character being aware of them.
e.g. "Her character kept touching her collar — a mannerism that showed anxiety even when the script gave no emotion."
In life: everyone has mannerisms that leak their emotional state — actors study them; psychologists, lawyers and employers read them
