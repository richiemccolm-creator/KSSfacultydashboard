S1Drama

S1 Drama introduces pupils to the fundamental building blocks of performance. Pupils develop core skills across mime, voice, improvisation, scripted work and production — growing in confidence, creativity and collaboration throughout the year.

The year culminates in a small-scale production where pupils take on performance or technical roles, applying everything they have learned in a real performance context.

Assessment& Tracking

Drama assessment at Knightswood is built around six Tracking Periods per year group. Each TP is tied to a specific unit, evidences named Experiences & Outcomes, and is assessed across four modes: Say, Write, Make, and Do.

Progress is recorded on a 1–4 scale. This data feeds into the school's BGE Tracking and Monitoring cycle.

How We Track
Structure, method, and recording
Tracking Periods
6 per year group
One per unit — aligned to the teaching calendar
Assessment Modes
Say · Write · Make · Do
Each TP specifies which modes apply to that unit
Progress Scale
1 — 4
1 Working on targets · 2 Not yet on track · 3 On track · 4 Above expectations
Recording

The 1–4 Scale
Applied across all four dimensions at each Tracking Period
1
Working on Personal Targets
Progress is below expected level for this stage. Individual support targets are in place and the pupil is working towards them with teacher guidance.
2
Not Yet on Track
Working towards CfE Level 3 expectations. Making progress but not yet consistently meeting the standard expected at this stage.
3
On Track
Meeting CfE Level 3 expectations. Progressing appropriately for stage and demonstrating the skills and knowledge expected.
4
Performing Above National Expectations
Exceeding CfE Level 3. Demonstrating extended skills, independence and depth of understanding beyond what is expected at this stage.

Faculty Context 2025–26

The Art & Drama Faculty continues to deliver outstanding outcomes and growth across all courses, with some of the highest attainment in Drama, Art & Design, and Photography in recent years. The sustained increase in pupil uptake at all levels demonstrates that young people value these subjects and recognise their relevance, creativity, and real-world application.

Faculty analysis and feedback show that learning and teaching remain key strengths, with staff providing engaging, inclusive experiences that build confidence, creativity, and critical thinking. This positive culture has directly contributed to the rise in attainment and pupil participation.

Through SQA analysis and internal moderation, two clear priorities for improvement have been identified — the Higher Drama written paper and the National 5 Art & Design (Design) component. These will form a major focus this session through targeted literacy and exam preparation strategies, moderation of written work, and exemplars of high-quality design process.

Alongside this, the faculty will build on the success of Photography by developing and repurposing creative spaces that support innovation, challenge high-attaining learners (Top 20%), and provide flexible areas for digital work and reflection.

Improve attendance and belonging through targeted support and inclusive practice.
Priority 02
Embed restorative approaches and UNCRC principles across all classrooms.
Priority 03
Strengthen literacy and meta-skills through improved pedagogy and assessment consistency.
Priority 04
Raise attainment — focus on Higher Drama written exams and N5 Design performance.
Priority 05
Enhance creative learning environments to support wellbeing and engagement.
Priority 06
Embed digital technologies — OneNote for evidence, Satchel One for homework and home communication.

Staff CLPL & Looking Inwards, Outwards and Forwards

Staff will engage in pedagogy pods, practitioner enquiry, and targeted digital CLPL (OneNote / SatchelOne) to improve consistency in planning, feedback, and evidence gathering. Faculty staff will also participate in SQA Understanding Standards events.

Looking Inwards
Evaluating our own practice through learning visits, moderation discussions, and departmental reflection. Staff analyse attainment data, lesson evidence, and pupil feedback to identify strengths and next steps.
Looking Outwards
Engaging with colleagues across other schools, local authority networks, and national SQA events. Visiting other schools to see how they deliver specific elements such as design processes and performance assessment.
Looking Forwards
Using new insights to plan next steps in pedagogy, assessment, and curriculum development — ensuring our practice evolves with national changes including revised Higher Drama exam structures and digital learning strategies.
