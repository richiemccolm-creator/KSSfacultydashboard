1. PURPOSE

At Knightswood Secondary School, we believe that effective communication with parents and carers is fundamental to raising attainment, supporting pupil wellbeing, and developing confident, successful learners. Tracking, monitoring, and reporting are key pillars in our strategy to ensure that all young people are supported and challenged appropriately through their Broad General Education (BGE) journey.

Each pupil receives:

  • One Interim Tracking Report
  • One Full Written Report per academic session.

These reports must:

  • Provide an accurate picture of the pupil’s current progress.
  • Celebrate strengths and successes.
  • Identify areas for development.
  • Offer clear, supportive next steps for improvement.

The purpose of this policy is to:

  • Establish a framework for staff to track, monitor, and report progress across their subject areas improving consistency across the school.
  • Align our practice with National guidelines
  • Promote equity, transparency, and collaboration between school and home
  • Ensure the system is manageable, sustainable, and meaningful for staff, pupils, and families.

2. TRACKING PUPILS: LEVELS AND PROFESSIONAL JUDGEMENT

Tracking at Knightswood is rooted in professional judgement and formative assessment, aligned with national standards and supported by moderation practices. Teachers assign each pupil a Curriculum for Excellence (CfE) working level, based on secure, consistent evidence of what they know, understand, and can do.

Expected National Levels (guidance only):

It is essential that tracking reflects the pupil’s current working level, based on secure, holistic, and ongoing evidence gathered through learning, teaching, and assessment.

Tracking is not determined by:

  • A pupil’s age or year group
  • Aspirations or predicted future outcomes
  • A single test or isolated piece of evidence

Tracking judgments must be based on a wide range of evidence, including but not limited to:

  • Observations of day-to-day learning
  • Discussions with pupils about their understanding
  • Classwork, practical tasks, and performances
  • Assessment tasks, where appropriate
  • Self and peer assessments

CHANGING LEVELS

Tracking must be responsive to evidence. If a pupil is not yet secure at a level (e.g., Third Level), and consistent evidence shows limited success, it may be appropriate to track them at a lower level (e.g., Second Level).

However, tracking down must be based on clear, holistic evidence, not just a lack of achievement at the higher level.

A pupil not meeting Third Level benchmarks does not automatically meet the criteria for Second Level. Teachers must use departmental guidance alongside their professional judgement and ensure there is secure evidence that the pupil is working consistently at the lower level before adjusting.

Tracking is not a fixed label. Levels should be revisited regularly as pupils develop new knowledge and skills through targeted support, adaptive and effective teaching.

3. MODERATION AND QUALITY ASSURANCE

1Plan learning, teaching & assessment
2Gather evidence from everyday learning
3Evaluate using Benchmarks & dialogue
4Moderate with colleagues
5Share standards in tracking & reporting

Tracking decisions at Knightswood Secondary School are supported by Education Scotland’s moderation cycle, which ensures consistent and evidence-based judgement-making. This cycle involves:

  • Planning Learning, Teaching and Assessment – Designing effective learning experiences and assessment tasks.
  • Gathering Evidence – Collecting a range of evidence from everyday classroom learning.
  • Evaluating Evidence – Using Benchmarks and professional dialogue to analyse the evidence.
  • Moderation and Professional Dialogue – Engaging in discussion with colleagues to agree standards.
  • Sharing Standards and Expectations – Reflecting shared understanding in tracking and reporting.

This cycle promotes accuracy, fairness, and consistency in assigning CfE levels across the school. Faculty and SLT moderation ensure consistency, fairness, and alignment with expectations.

4. REPORTING STRUCTURE

Interim report

Snapshot of progress and engagement · no narrative comments.

Full written report

One per session · strengths, development areas, and clear next steps for parents.

Interim Reports:

  • Provide a snapshot of progress and engagement.
  • Do not include narrative comments.

Full Written Reports follow a consistent structure:

Section

Content

Level and Progress

Clearly state the pupil’s current Curriculum for Excellence (CfE) Working Level and their Progress Rating.

Strengths and Achievements

Highlight key learning skills, positive behaviours, and notable recent achievements across curricular areas. Focus on specific strengths, using clear and accessible language.

Areas for Development

Identify up to two specific skills, knowledge areas, or behaviours where further progress is needed. Use constructive, supportive language.

Next Steps

Set a clear, realistic learning target or action that the pupil can work towards over the coming term. Targets should be achievable and framed positively.

5. LEARNER CONVERSATIONS AND PUPIL OWNERSHIP

Tracking and reporting should feed directly into learner conversations, helping pupils reflect on their learning and take increased responsibility for their progress.

These conversations can occur in class tasks but should always be carried out at tracking points.

The conversations should be recorded on the departmental template and young people should take a photo of this on their iPad. These will be used in PSE classes to discuss and plan next steps across their subjects.

7. USING TRACKING TO SUPPORT INTERVENTIONS & OPTION CHOICES

Initial intervention should begin at class teacher level. This includes identifying concerns early, adjusting classroom practice, and engaging in direct discussion with the learner.

Where further support is required, the classroom teacher must discuss the case with the Faculty Head or Principal Teacher. This ensures:

  • A shared understanding of the pupil’s needs
  • Alignment of support strategies across classes
  • Consistency and accuracy in tracking judgements
  • Clear communication of next steps

Effective intervention depends on collaboration between classroom teachers, Faculty Heads/Principal Teachers, Pastoral Care, and SLT. Interventions should be tracked internally and referenced in follow-up tracking entries.

Key tracking information — including CfE working levels, progress ratings, effort, behaviour, and home learning — will be used by:

  • Pastoral Care staff during option choice meetings and learner reviews and target setting.
  • SLT when monitoring cohort progress
  • Faculties when advising pupils and parents about subject suitability and course entry requirements

Option choice conversations must be informed by a balance of professional judgement, tracking data, and learner voice.

Tracking information should highlight where early interventions or additional support may be required to enable pupils to access desired pathways, including National Qualifications and wider achievement awards.

Using tracking in this way strengthens the link between learning, assessment, and progression, ensuring that every pupil moves into the Senior Phase with a clear, supported, and ambitious learning plan.
