# Unit 2 — Creative Industries: Skills Development
## N5 Skills for Work: Creative Industries (J17W 75)
### Lesson Outlines — Sep to Oct 2025

**Unit colour:** Acid Green (#AAFF00)
**Sessions:** 3 × double periods per week — Tuesday, Wednesday, Friday
**Double period = 100 minutes (2 × 50 min)**
**Teaching weeks in U2:** Weeks 5–9 (8 Sep to 10 Oct), overlapping with U1 NAB window
**NAB window:** Weeks 8–9 alongside U1 (29 Sep to 10 Oct)

**NAB outcomes to evidence:**
- O1: Investigate practical skills required for a chosen job role (PCs a, b, c)
- O2: Develop own practical skills (PCs a, b, c) — THIS IS THE MAIN FOCUS
- O3: Evaluate own practical skills development (PCs a, b, c)

**Key NAB notes:**
- O2 PC(b) and PC(c) require an **assessor observation checklist** — this must be completed during practical sessions
- Practical evidence is the primary assessment vehicle — not written work
- Candidates must demonstrate on at least one occasion: selecting appropriate resources/techniques/equipment; creatively using them; developing creative expression
- O3 requires feedback from **two sources** — teacher/lecturer AND one other person (peer or external observer)

**Curriculum links (across all U2 lessons):**
- Core Skills: Critical Thinking (O1 skills audit), Planning & Organising (O2 planning), Working Co-operatively with Others (peer feedback throughout)
- CfE Capacities: Successful Learners, Confident Individuals, Effective Contributors
- NOS: CPD1 (improving own practical skills), PD1 (production brief awareness), DES6 (working with others on creative tasks)

## IMPORTANT: HOW U2 WORKS

U2 is fundamentally different from U1. Where U1 is largely research and writing-based, U2 is **primarily practical**. The SQA guidance is explicit: the majority of delivery time should be dedicated to practical activity. The written elements (skills audit, plan, evaluation) exist to frame and evidence the practical work — they are not the point.

Each learner chooses a **job role** in their chosen Creative Industries sector and spends the unit developing three practical skills associated with that role. The sector and job role from U1 naturally feed into this — learners may use the same role they researched, or choose a different one. They should also be encouraged to think ahead to U4 (Creative Project) where they will take on a role within a team — the skills they develop now should be relevant to that.

**The practical sessions are the heart of this unit.** Your job in most sessions is to circulate, observe, challenge, support and complete observation checklists — not to deliver content from the front.

## WEEK 5 — 8–12 Sep 2025

### Tue 9 Sep — Session 11: U2 Launch + Skills Audit (O1 PCs a & b)
**Duration:** 100 minutes
**Learning intention:** Understand what U2 involves. Choose a job role. Identify required practical skills and honestly evaluate your own against them.

| Time | Activity |
|------|----------|
| 0–15 | **U2 introduction.** Explain how this unit works — it's primarily practical, the doing is the main event, written work frames the doing not the other way round. Show the three outcomes clearly. Explain the observation checklist — what it is, when it happens, what you'll be looking for. Demystify the assessment. |
| 15–30 | **Job role selection.** Learners choose the job role they will focus on for U2. This may be the same role they researched in U1 or a different one. Prompt them to think ahead: what role might they play in the U4 Creative Project? The practical skills they develop now should be useful then. Teacher works around the room confirming choices. |
| 30–60 | **Practical skills identification (NAB O1 PC a).** Learners research and identify a minimum of three practical skills required for their chosen job role. Sources: internet, job specs found during U1, published materials. Skills must be specific and practical — not "communication skills" but "operating a lighting desk", "hand-sewing costume alterations", "using Adobe Premiere Pro to cut a sequence". |
| 60–85 | **Skills self-evaluation (NAB O1 PC b).** Learners evaluate their own current level against each identified skill using the rating scale: little/no experience, some experience, lots of experience. Honest self-assessment with a brief comment explaining each rating. Encourage them to be realistic — this is their starting point, not a judgement. |
| 85–100 | **Initial peer feedback.** Learners share their skills list and self-evaluation with a partner. Partner gives honest verbal feedback: does the rating seem accurate based on what they know of this person? Any skills missing? Learner notes any adjustments. |

**Evidence generated:** Skills identification + self-evaluation → NAB O1 PC(a) and PC(b)
**NAB criteria met:** O1 PC(a) — three practical skills identified; O1 PC(b) — self-evaluation completed
**Assessor action:** Check that skills identified are genuinely practical and specific to the job role. Challenge anything too vague.

### Wed 10 Sep — Session 12: Targets + Development Plan (O1 PC c + O2 PCs a & b)
**Duration:** 100 minutes
**Learning intention:** Set development targets. Identify approaches and produce a brief plan for developing practical skills.

| Time | Activity |
|------|----------|
| 0–20 | **Setting targets (NAB O1 PC c).** Learners set a minimum of three specific targets for developing their practical skills, based on their self-evaluation. Targets must be specific and achievable — not "get better at photography" but "learn how to control depth of field using aperture settings" or "learn to use a sewing machine to produce a French seam". Teacher challenges vague targets — push for precision. |
| 20–50 | **Approaches and plan (NAB O2 PCs a & b).** Learners identify at least three approaches they will use to develop their skills and produce a brief plan for each — covering who can help them, what they need, how they will do it, where, and when it must be done by. The SQA example (costume designer visiting a theatre, practising on sewing machines, researching designers' sketches) is a useful model to share. Plans should be realistic and show genuine thought about how they will actually make progress. |
| 50–70 | **Resources, techniques and equipment.** For each approach, learners identify the specific resources, techniques and equipment they will need. This feeds O2 PC(a) and (b). Teacher pushes for specificity — not just "camera" but "DSLR with 50mm lens and external flash unit". |
| 70–90 | **Plan peer review.** Learners swap plans with a partner. Partner reviews: Is this plan realistic? Are the approaches likely to actually develop the skills? Is anything missing? Written feedback given. Learner revises plan if needed. |
| 90–100 | **Plan sign-off and practical session preview.** Teacher reviews and signs off plans that are ready. Explain that from next session onwards the majority of time is practical — they need to arrive ready to work. Brief discussion: what do they need to bring or prepare? |

**Evidence generated:** Three targets → NAB O1 PC(c); Approaches + plan → NAB O2 PC(a) and PC(b)
**NAB criteria met:** O1 PC(c) — targets set; O2 PC(a) — approaches selected; O2 PC(b) begins — plan produced
**Assessor action:** Sign off completed plans. Note which learners need resources arranged in advance.

### Fri 12 Sep — Session 13: Practical Skills Development #1 (O2 PC b & c)
**Duration:** 100 minutes
**Learning intention:** Begin implementing planned approaches. Practise and develop practical skills in a creative context.

| Time | Activity |
|------|----------|
| 0–10 | **Session briefing.** Recap plans — what is each learner working on today? What are they aiming to achieve in this session? Learners write a brief session intention: "Today I am going to work on [skill] using [approach/resource]." This takes 5 minutes and focuses them immediately. |
| 10–90 | **Practical skills development — open working time.** Learners work on their chosen practical skills using their planned approaches. The room may look different for each learner — one is at a computer working in design software, another is with instruments, another is working on performance skills. This is intentional and correct. Teacher circulates continuously: observe, support, challenge, stretch. Begin completing **assessor observation checklists** for learners who are demonstrating the required behaviours — selecting appropriate resources/techniques/equipment, creatively using them, developing creative expression. Not every learner will be ready for a checklist in session 1 — that's fine. |
| 90–100 | **Session reflection.** Learners write 3–4 sentences in their folio: what did I work on, what progress did I make, what will I focus on next session. Quick, habitual, not laboured. This builds into O3 evidence later. |

**Evidence generated:** Practical work (products, recordings, photos, teacher observation) → NAB O2 PC(b)(c); session reflections (O3 prep)
**NAB criteria met:** O2 PC(b) and PC(c) — observation checklist started
**Assessor action:** Complete observation checklists for any learner who has clearly demonstrated the three required behaviours. Photograph practical work where possible as evidence.

### Tue 16 Sep — Session 14: Practical Skills Development #2
**Duration:** 100 minutes
**Learning intention:** Continue developing practical skills. Experiment and take creative risks.

| Time | Activity |
|------|----------|
| 0–10 | **Starter: Session intention.** Learners review last session's reflection and write today's intention. What specifically are they working on and what do they want to achieve? |
| 10–85 | **Practical skills development — open working time.** Continued practical work on chosen skills and approaches. Teacher circulates: push learners who are playing it safe to try something harder or more experimental. This is the session to encourage creative risk-taking — trying a technique that might not work, experimenting with an unfamiliar piece of equipment, pushing beyond the comfortable. Complete observation checklists for any learners not yet checked off. |
| 85–100 | **Mid-point peer feedback.** Learners share their practical work with a partner and receive verbal feedback. What's working well? What could be pushed further? Brief written note of feedback received — this begins building the O3 peer feedback evidence. |

**Evidence generated:** Continued practical work + peer feedback notes → O2 PC(b)(c); O3 PC(b) begins
**NAB criteria met:** O2 PC(b)(c) continued; O3 PC(b) — peer feedback initiated
**Assessor action:** Continue observation checklists. Note any learners who have now demonstrated all three required behaviours.

### Wed 17 Sep — Session 15: Practical Skills Development #3
**Duration:** 100 minutes
**Learning intention:** Develop and refine skills further. Begin to show evidence of creative expression.

| Time | Activity |
|------|----------|
| 0–10 | **Starter: Session intention + quick show.** Learners write their session intention, then briefly show the group what they produced last session. Fast — 30 seconds each maximum. Builds accountability and generates peer interest. |
| 10–85 | **Practical skills development — open working time.** Focus this session on **refinement** — not just doing but improving. Encourage learners to look critically at their own work: what isn't working? What would a professional do differently here? Teacher works in depth with two or three learners who need more targeted support, while others work independently. |
| 85–100 | **Teacher feedback round.** Teacher gives brief verbal feedback to each learner on their progress so far. Honest, specific and encouraging — not just "that's good". What has genuinely improved? What still needs work? Learners note feedback in folio. |

**Evidence generated:** Refined practical work; teacher feedback noted → O2 PC(b)(c); O3 PC(b)
**NAB criteria met:** O2 PC(b)(c) continued; O3 PC(b) — teacher feedback recorded

### Fri 19 Sep — Session 16: Practical Skills Development #4 + External Input
**Duration:** 100 minutes
**Learning intention:** Continue practical development. Seek and use feedback from beyond the classroom where possible.

| Time | Activity |
|------|----------|
| 0–10 | **Starter: Session intention.** As previous sessions. |
| 10–70 | **Practical skills development — open working time.** Continued practical work. By this point learners should be showing clear development from their starting point. Teacher challenges learners to evidence this — can they demonstrate the same skill they started with but at a noticeably higher level? |
| 70–90 | **External feedback opportunity.** If a visiting professional, another teacher from a relevant subject, or a more senior learner can be arranged, this is the ideal session for them to observe and give feedback. **Evidence generated:** Practical work continued; external/peer feedback recorded → O2 PC(b)(c); O3 PC(b) — second feedback source
**NAB criteria met:** O2 PC(b)(c); O3 PC(b) — two sources of feedback collected if external arranged
**Note:** If external feedback isn't possible this week, it can be arranged in week 7. Document who gave feedback and when.

### Tue 23 Sep — Session 17: Practical Skills Development #5
**Duration:** 100 minutes
**Learning intention:** Push practical skills to their current limit. Produce the strongest possible evidence of creative development.

| Time | Activity |
|------|----------|
| 0–10 | **Progress review.** Learners look back at their original self-evaluation and targets from week 5. How far have they come? Rate themselves again against the same skills. Where have they made most progress? Where is there still work to do? 10 minutes of honest reflection. |
| 10–85 | **Practical skills development — final push.** This is the last substantial practical session before consolidation begins. Learners should be working at their best — producing the strongest, most creative, most refined piece of practical work they can. Teacher completes any outstanding observation checklists this session — by end of this session every learner should have a completed checklist. |
| 85–100 | **Peer showcase.** Learners briefly present or demonstrate their practical work to the group. Informal — 2 minutes each maximum. Group gives one positive comment each. Builds confidence and gives learners a sense of their own progress. |

**Evidence generated:** Final practical work; completed observation checklists → O2 PC(b)(c) — complete
**NAB criteria met:** O2 all PCs
**Assessor action:** All observation checklists must be completed by end of this session. Photograph or record practical work where appropriate.

### Wed 24 Sep — Session 18: O3 Evaluation (O3 PCs a, b, c)
**Duration:** 100 minutes
**Learning intention:** Produce a full evaluation of your practical skills development across the unit.

| Time | Activity |
|------|----------|
| 0–15 | **Evaluation briefing.** Walk through the O3 criteria — what a good evaluation actually looks like. Share the SQA example response (costume designer example) as a model. Emphasise that this is about honest, specific reflection — not a list of what they did but a genuine analysis of what worked, what didn't, and why. |
| 15–80 | **O3 evaluation writing time (NAB O3 PCs a, b, c).** Learners produce their evaluation covering: (1) which approaches worked well and why, (2) which approaches didn't work well and why, (3) whether the plan was helpful, (4) what the main points of feedback were from others, (5) which targets they developed most successfully and why. This is open-book — learners use their session reflections, feedback notes and folio throughout. Teacher circulates and challenges surface-level responses: "You said the approach worked well — can you be more specific about what it gave you?" |
| 80–100 | **Peer review of evaluation.** Learners swap evaluations and give written feedback: Is it specific enough? Does it honestly address what didn't work? Is the feedback from others clearly referenced? Learner makes any final improvements. |

**Evidence generated:** Full written evaluation → NAB O3 PC(a)(b)(c) — complete
**NAB criteria met:** O3 all PCs
**Note:** Evaluation can be written, oral or presented in another format if appropriate. Written is most common and most straightforward to evidence.

## Note on Differentiation Across U2

U2 is the most naturally differentiated unit in the course because every learner is working on their own chosen skills at their own starting point. The role of the teacher is to calibrate challenge individually:

- **Learners who are already strong in their practical area** should be pushed to extend beyond their comfort zone — try more complex techniques, more ambitious outcomes, work to a higher standard of finish.
- **Learners with little or no experience** in their chosen area need more structured support and scaffolding in the early sessions, with the aim of building genuine confidence before the observation checklist.
- **Learners who struggle with the written elements** (targets, plan, evaluation) can complete these orally with teacher scribing or using audio recording — SQA explicitly permits this.
