Warm Up  ·  Explore

Name & Gesture Circle

How to Play
  • 1
    Stand in a circle as a class
  • 2
    The first person says their name and does a gesture that represents them
  • 3
    The whole group copies the name and gesture together
  • 4
    Move clockwise until everyone has had a turn
  • 5
    Final round: can you remember everyone's name and gesture?

Unit 01
Key Vocabulary 12 / 17
Key Vocabulary  ·  Movement

Use these words in your notes and verbal evaluations.

BL
Body Language
Communicating non-verbally through the position and movement of the body.
e.g. Crossed arms shows defensiveness or low status
EC
Eye Contact
Using direct gaze to show relationships, tension, or emotion between characters.
e.g. Avoiding eye contact shows fear or submission
UL
Use of Levels
Using different heights on stage to show status and create visual variety.
e.g. Standing above others suggests power
PX
Proxemics
The use of space and distance between performers to communicate relationships.
e.g. Standing close suggests intimacy or threat
PS
Posture
How you stand and carry your body to represent character or status.
e.g. Slouching suggests low status or laziness
TM
Timing
The precision of when a movement or line is delivered for maximum effect.
e.g. A pause before speaking can build tension
Unit 01
Key Vocabulary 13 / 17
Key Vocabulary  ·  Dramatic Features

Wider Drama vocabulary to use in evaluation and discussion.

MA
Mood & Atmosphere
Mood is the tone or feeling of the drama. Atmosphere is the feeling the audience gets from watching.
ST
Status
How much power a character has — shown through levels, eye contact, proximity and posture.
FF
Freeze Frame
A stage picture held without movement — a frozen image that represents a moment or idea.
