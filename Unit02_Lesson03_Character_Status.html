NEW VOCABULARY· Learn these two terms — you will need them today and all unit Lesson 3
Two Key Terms
STATUS
How much power and importance a character has — shown through levels, eye contact, proximity and posture. Status is not about how loud you are. It's about how you hold space.
High status: upright, still, holds your gaze.
Low status: collapsed, avoids eye contact, shrinks.
PROXEMICS
The use of space and distance between performers to communicate relationships and power. How close you stand to someone — or how far away — changes everything the audience reads about your relationship.
Close = intimacy, threat or conflict.
Far = distance, fear, avoidance.
QUICK CHALLENGE — WITHOUT MOVING FROM WHERE YOU ARE
Change your proxemics and status right now. Show the person next to you that you are low status. Now high status. What changed?

Status Walk

The Interview
1
In pairs. No words — entirely in mime. One person is the high-status interviewer, one is the nervous applicant.
2
Perform for 2 minutes. Then swap roles. Perform again.
3
Then reverse the status entirely — the applicant is arrogant, the interviewer is nervous. How does the whole scene change?

Which S.P.E.C.S. elements changed most between high and low status? What specifically changed in your body — and why did it work?

ACTIVITY· Character Walks — stay in character no matter what happens 22–40 min
22–40
minutes
Character Walks
Tired Old Professor
Heavy steps. Slight stoop. Stops to think mid-movement. Adjusts glasses. Carries the weight of years of knowledge.
Status: 6. Respected but tired.
Excited Child
Light, bouncy, unpredictable. Everything is fascinating. Can't stay still. Reacts to absolutely everything in the room.
Status: 3. No power, pure energy.
Suspicious Detective
Measured steps. Eyes scan everything. Stops, observes, moves on. Nothing escapes notice. Gives nothing away.
Status: 8. Controlled authority.
Proud Soldier
Perfectly upright. Chest out, chin raised. Every step deliberate. Commands the space around them absolutely.
Status: 9. Rigid discipline.
1
Walk the space as your character. When your teacher narrates a scenario — react in character. Stay in it. Never break.
2
Scenarios: You receive a letter… You drop something… You meet an old friend… You must make a decision.

ACTIVITY· Frozen Moment — groups of 5, then class identifies 40–45 min
40–45
minutes
Frozen Moment
1
In groups of 4–5. Each person is a different character from today.
2
When your teacher says freeze — hold a moment that shows all characters clearly in one still image.
3
The rest of the class must identify who each person is — and which S.P.E.C.S. elements gave it away.
4
Think carefully about: levels, proxemics, posture, eye contact, and the direction you are facing.
CLASS — WHEN WATCHING, BE READY TO NAME
Who is the highest status in the image — and how can you tell from the body alone?
A frozen image should communicate everything in one glance. If you have to look twice — it isn't clear enough yet.
