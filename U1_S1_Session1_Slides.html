U01 / S01 / ASSESSMENT
HOW YOU ARE ASSESSED
NO EXAM.
You build a folio of evidence across the year. Each unit has a NAB — tasks that confirm you've met the outcomes. Most evidence comes from the work you do in class every day.
Written tasks · Practical work · Research · Presentations · Self-evaluations
U01 / S01 / ASSESSMENT
NATIONAL ASSESSMENT BANK
WHAT IS A NAB?
01
A NAB is a set of tasks that confirms you have met the outcomes for a unit.
02
It's open-book. You use your folio and notes. It's not a memory test.
03
Most of your evidence is already built through classwork. The NAB formalises it.
There are four units, so there are four NABs — one per unit. No surprises.

U01 / S01 / SELF-EVAL
HOW TO COMPLETE YOUR SELF-EVALUATION
FOUR STEPS
01
Read each skill carefully
Understand what the skill actually means before you rate yourself
02
Rate yourself honestly — 1 (not there yet) to 5 (confident)
Rating yourself 5 on everything isn't impressive — it's not useful
03
Write a comment — use a real example from your life
Not from school if possible — from sport, part-time work, family, hobbies
04
Identify one skill you most want to develop this year
This will become your focus — be honest about what you actually want to improve
