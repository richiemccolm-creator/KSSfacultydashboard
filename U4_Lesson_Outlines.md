## HOW THIS UNIT WORKS

U4 is the capstone of the course. Everything learners have done across U1, U2 and U3 feeds directly into this unit — the sector knowledge from U1, the practical skills from U2, and the creative process experience from U3 are all being deployed here in a sustained team project.

The SQA guidance is explicit: **the majority of delivery time should be dedicated to Outcome 2 — implementation**. The planning (O1) should be thorough but efficient. Don't let it drag. Get them into the doing as quickly as possible.

**Three outcomes:**
- **O1** — Contribute to planning the project (all performance evidence — observation checklist)
- **O2** — Contribute to implementing the project (all performance evidence — observation checklist, with one written progress review)
- **O3** — Evaluate the project (written/oral evidence)

**Both O1 and O2 are entirely observation-checklist based.** There is no written NAB task for either — the group plan document supports the checklist but the checklist is the evidence. This means your observation of team planning meetings and implementation sessions is the primary assessment activity throughout the unit.

**The brief:** You issue the brief at the start of the unit. It should be open enough for teams to interpret creatively but specific enough to give real parameters. The SQA example (promotional materials for a school event) is fine, but a more contemporary brief — a community campaign, a digital publication, a showcase event, a content package for a local organisation — will generate more authentic engagement. The brief must include: client/target audience, background, requirements, considerations/constraints, client expectations, and what a successful project looks like.

**Teams:** Organise learners into teams of 3–5 before the unit begins. Consider sector alignment, skills balance and interpersonal dynamics. Teams should ideally be small enough that every member's contribution is visible and meaningful.

**The O3 Self-Evaluation #3:** The third and final employability self-evaluation from U1 O3 sits in this unit — the SQA guidance recommends it falls at the end of the course. Build it into week 22.

## BEFORE WEEK 19 — Teacher Preparation

Before January, have the following ready:

- **The brief** — written in proper SQA brief format, appropriate for your cohort and sector mix. Consider whether one brief works for the whole class or whether different teams work from briefs tailored to their sector.
- **Teams confirmed** — 3–5 learners per team, pre-allocated. Don't waste the first session on team formation drama.
- **Observation checklists printed** — one per learner for O1, one per learner for O2. You will be completing these throughout weeks 19–23.
- **Group plan template** — either the SQA template or your own version, ready to issue.
- **Review dates set** — build in at least one formal mid-project progress review (O2 PC d) around week 21/22.

### Tue 6 Jan — Session 47: Brief Issue + Initial Team Discussion (O1 PCs a, b, c, d, e, f)
**Duration:** 100 minutes
**Learning intention:** Receive and analyse the brief. Begin team planning discussion. Start working towards a group plan.

| Time | Activity |
|------|----------|
| 0–10 | **Welcome back + unit overview.** Brief overview of how U4 works — team project, observation-based assessment, planning then doing, evaluation at the end. Remind learners that everything they've learned across the year is now being put to use. Introduce the teams. |
| 10–20 | **Brief reading and analysis.** Teams receive the brief and read it carefully together. Each learner annotates their copy: what is being asked? What are the constraints? What does success look like? What is still unclear? |
| 20–30 | **Class brief discussion.** Any questions about the brief answered. Clarify constraints. If there are multiple briefs for different teams, each team presents their brief to the class — why it suits their sector and skills. |
| 30–90 | **Team planning meeting #1 — OBSERVATION CHECKLIST (O1 PCs a–f).** Teams begin their planning discussion. Teacher circulates between teams, observing and completing O1 checklists. Listen for: genuine contribution to decisions (not one person dominating), creative ideas, realistic thinking, awareness of resources and constraints. Prompt teams who are stuck: "What's the first thing that needs to happen?" "Who is best placed to do that?" "How long do you think that will realistically take?" Teams should be working towards: project idea/title agreed (PC a), key tasks listed (PC b), resources identified (PC c), roles beginning to emerge (PC d). |
| 90–100 | **End of session check-in.** Each team states where they've got to and what they need to resolve at Wednesday's session. Teacher flags any teams who are struggling to reach agreement — intervene before Wednesday if needed. |

**Evidence generated:** O1 observation checklist — PCs (a)(b)(c) in progress; planning discussion observed
**NAB criteria met:** O1 PC(a)(b)(c) — observation checklist begun
**Assessor action:** Begin O1 checklists for all learners. Note who is contributing meaningfully and who is hanging back — address in Wednesday's session.

### Wed 7 Jan — Session 48: Group Plan Completion (O1 PCs d, e, f)
**Duration:** 100 minutes
**Learning intention:** Complete the group plan. Agree and document roles, own tasks and timescales.

| Time | Activity |
|------|----------|
| 0–15 | **Team planning meeting #2 — OBSERVATION CHECKLIST continues (O1 PCs d, e, f).** Teams pick up where they left off. Teacher completes remaining O1 checklist PCs — focusing now on: allocation of roles and responsibilities (PC d), each learner agreeing their own specific tasks (PC e), timescales and review dates agreed (PC f). |
| 15–60 | **Group plan document production.** Teams formally document their plan using the group plan template: project idea/title, key tasks, resources, roles and responsibilities (each member), own tasks per member, timescales for key stages, agreed review dates. Teacher reviews draft plans — is the project achievable? Are the timescales realistic? Are tasks specific enough? Sign off plans that are ready. Push back on any that are too vague or over-ambitious. |
| 60–80 | **Individual task planning.** Each learner produces a brief individual plan for their own role: what do I need to do, how will I do it, what resources do I need, what is my personal deadline. This sits alongside the group plan in the folio. |
| 80–100 | **Plan sign-off and implementation preview.** Teacher signs off completed group plans. Explain that from Friday the focus shifts entirely to doing — the plan is the guide, not a document to keep revising. Learners review their individual tasks and identify anything they need to prepare before Friday. |

**Evidence generated:** Completed group plan → O1 PCs (a)–(f) documented; individual task plan
**NAB criteria met:** O1 all PCs — observation checklist completed; group plan in folio
**Assessor action:** Complete and sign O1 observation checklists for all learners by end of this session. Sign off group plans. Flag any learner whose contribution to planning was insufficient — a catch-up planning conversation may be needed.

### Fri 9 Jan — Session 49: Implementation Begins (O2 PCs a, b, c, e)
**Duration:** 100 minutes
**Learning intention:** Begin implementing the creative project. Carry out agreed tasks. Work safely and support team members.

| Time | Activity |
|------|----------|
| 0–10 | **Session briefing.** Each learner states their planned task for today — specific, not vague. "I'm going to..." not "I'll work on my part." This takes 5 minutes and creates immediate focus and accountability. |
| 10–85 | **Implementation — open working time (O2 PCs a, b, c, e — OBSERVATION CHECKLIST begins).** Learners begin carrying out their agreed tasks. The room will look different for each team — some making, some filming, some designing, some writing. Teacher circulates between teams completing O2 checklists: is the learner carrying out their planned tasks (PC a)? Are they using appropriate skills, resources, techniques and equipment (PC b)? Are they working safely (PC c)? Are they actively supporting team members when needed (PC e)? Note: H&S should be explicitly discussed with every team in this first implementation session — what are the relevant guidelines for their specific activity? |
| 85–100 | **Session log.** Each learner writes a brief session log entry: what did I do today, what progress did I make, what will I do next session. This becomes a running log that feeds O2 PC(d) and O3. Quick, habitual, not laboured. |

**Evidence generated:** Practical work; session log; O2 observation checklist begun
**NAB criteria met:** O2 PC(a)(b)(c)(e) — checklist in progress
**Assessor action:** Begin O2 checklists. Have explicit H&S conversation with each team about their specific activity.
