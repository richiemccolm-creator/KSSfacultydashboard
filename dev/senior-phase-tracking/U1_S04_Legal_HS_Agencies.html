U01 · S04 · LEGAL PARAMETERS
KEY LEGAL AREAS — CLICK TO REVEAL
LEGAL
PARAMETERS
COPYRIGHT
Protects original work — music, writing, photography, design, film.
Belongs to the creator automatically. Lasts 70 years after death.
INTELLECTUAL PROPERTY (IP)
Broader than copyright — covers trademarks, patents, design rights.
Protects brand names, logos, inventions, unique designs.
CONTRACTS
A legal agreement between two parties — performer and venue, designer and client, etc.
Sets out pay, deliverables, timescales, rights.
LICENSING
Permission to use someone else's work — music in a film, a font in a design, an image in a publication.
Without a licence, it's infringement.
GDPR / DATA PROTECTION
Controls how personal data is collected, stored and used.
Relevant to any creative business holding client information.
IMAGE RIGHTS
A person's right to control how their image is used commercially.
Especially relevant in photography, film, advertising, social media.
Take notes in your own words as we go — this becomes your PC(b) evidence.

U01 · S04 · H&S LEGISLATION
KEY H&S LEGISLATION — CLICK TO REVEAL
HEALTH &
SAFETY
HEALTH & SAFETY AT WORK ACT 1974
The main piece of H&S law in the UK.
Employers must provide a safe working environment. Workers must cooperate with safety measures.
MANUAL HANDLING REGULATIONS 1992
Covers lifting, carrying, pushing, pulling.
Relevant for: technicians, riggers, touring crew, set builders — anyone moving equipment.
NOISE AT WORK REGULATIONS 2005
Protects workers from hearing damage caused by loud noise.
Highly relevant for: musicians, sound engineers, venue staff, DJs.
COSHH REGULATIONS 2002
Control of Substances Hazardous to Health.
Relevant for: art and design (solvents, paints), technical theatre (pyrotechnics, chemicals).
RISK ASSESSMENT
A legal requirement — identify hazards, assess risks, put controls in place.
Required before any production, shoot, performance or event.
WORKING AT HEIGHT REGULATIONS 2005
Covers any work above ground level.
Highly relevant for: technical theatre (rigging, lighting), film production (cranes, elevated rigs).
Note the ones most relevant to your sector — this is your PC(c) evidence.

Creative Scotland
National arts funding body. Distributes funding to artists, companies and organisations across all creative sectors in Scotland.
Screen Scotland

Equity
Trade union for performers — actors, dancers, singers, stage managers. Negotiates pay, contracts and working conditions.

Champions the value of design across the UK — policy, education, business support for designers.
