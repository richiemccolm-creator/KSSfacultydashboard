U01 · S06 · FREELANCE ISSUES
EXAMPLES — TO HELP YOUR THINKING
WHAT
COUNTS AS
AN ISSUE?
IRREGULAR INCOME
No guaranteed monthly salary — income depends on bookings and contracts.
e.g. A freelance photographer may earn well in summer but struggle in January.
NO SICK PAY
If you can't work, you don't get paid. No employer to cover you.
e.g. A freelance dancer who injures themselves loses all income immediately.
FINDING CLIENTS
You must market yourself constantly — no employer to bring work to you.
e.g. A self-employed graphic designer must build and maintain a client base.
SELF-ASSESSMENT TAX
Freelancers must manage their own tax returns — no employer to handle it.
e.g. A session musician must track all earnings and submit an annual tax return.
NO EMPLOYMENT RIGHTS
No holiday pay, redundancy pay or formal employment protections.
e.g. A freelance filmmaker can have a contract cancelled with no compensation.
OWN EQUIPMENT COSTS
Freelancers often must buy and maintain their own professional equipment.
e.g. A freelance sound engineer may need to invest thousands in their own kit.
Pick the two most relevant to your sector. Make them specific — not generic.
