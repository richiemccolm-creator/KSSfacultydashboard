Key Vocabulary
Body Language
Communicating non-verbally through the position and movement of the body.
Eye Contact
Using direct gaze to show relationships, power or emotion between performers.
Posture
How you stand and carry your body to show relationships and emotion between characters.
Use of Levels
Using different heights on stage to show relationships and create visual variety.
Proxemics
The use of space and distance between performers to communicate relationships.NEW TODAY
Timing
The precision of when a movement or action is delivered — everyone must move or freeze together.NEW TODAY

CREATE· Groups of 4–5 22–44 min
22–44

Trust Exercises
Mirror Work
Face your partner and copy their movements exactly. Take turns to lead. No touching.
Blind Walk
One person closes their eyes. Their partner guides them safely around the space using voice only.
Levels Walk
Walk around the space. Use levels, posture and eye contact to show different relationships — who leads, who follows, who stands apart.
Remember: safety first · listen to your partner · use Drama vocabulary in your debrief
