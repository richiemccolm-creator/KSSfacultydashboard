By the end of this unit, we will understand how Drama works and why working together matters.

What is Drama?

Drama is…
  • A way of communicating through performance, movement and voice
  • A space to explore ideas, emotions and stories safely
  • A collaborative art form — it needs everyone working together
  • A skill that builds confidence, empathy and creativity

Why It Matters

"Drama teaches you to listen, to look, to trust and to take risks — skills you need everywhere in life."
— Expressive Arts Faculty, Knightswood

Ground Rules

Drama Rules & Safe Practices

L
Listen when others are speaking — no interrupting
S
Stay in your own space — respect others' boundaries
V
Voice level appropriate to the task at all times
T
Take creative risks — mistakes are part of learning
R
Respond quickly and calmly to teacher instructions

Why It Matters

"A Drama room only works when everyone feels safe enough to take risks and make mistakes."
— Expressive Arts Faculty

Create  ·  Warm Up

Name & Gesture Circle

How to Play
1
Stand in a circle as a class
2
Say your name and do a gesture that represents you
3
The whole group copies your name and gesture together
4
Move clockwise until everyone has had a turn
5
Final round — can you remember everyone's name and gesture?

Focus On

Eye Contact with the group
Clear, confident Gesture
Listening & Watching others
Energy — commit to the gesture!
