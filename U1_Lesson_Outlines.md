# Unit 1 — Creative Industries: An Introduction
## N5 Skills for Work: Creative Industries (J17V 75)
### Lesson Outlines — Aug to early Oct 2025

**Unit colour:** Electric Blue (#00AAFF)
**Sessions:** 3 × double periods per week — Tuesday, Wednesday, Friday
**Double period = 100 minutes (2 × 50 min)**
**Teaching weeks in U1:** Weeks 1–8 (mid-Aug to early Oct), NAB window weeks 8–9

**NAB outcomes to evidence:**
- O1: Investigate the Creative Industries and a chosen sector (PCs a, b, c, d)
- O2: Investigate employment within a chosen sector (PCs a, b, c, d)
- O3: Review and evaluate own employability skills (3 × self-eval reviews across whole course — Reviews 1 and 2 sit in U1)

**Curriculum links (across all U1 lessons):**
- Core Skills: Critical Thinking (O1), Planning & Organising (O3), Working Co-operatively with Others (O2 presentations)
- CfE Capacities: Successful Learners, Confident Individuals, Responsible Citizens, Effective Contributors
- NOS: CPD1 (improving own learning), DES18 (understanding industry context and briefs)

### Fri 15 Aug — Session 1: Course Induction + Self-Eval #1
**Duration:** 100 minutes
**Learning intention:** Understand what the course involves, what the year looks like, and establish a baseline for your own employability skills.

| Time | Activity |
|------|----------|
| 0–15 | **Welcome & icebreaker.** Learners introduce themselves and name one creative thing they do, watch, make or listen to. No wrong answers. Establishes that creativity is already part of their lives before the course has even started. |
| 15–35 | **Course overview.** Walk through the four units, what the year looks like (show the Gantt chart), how assessment works — no exam, folio + NABs — and a teaser for the Go Viral challenge in term 2. Get them excited about what's coming. |
| 35–55 | **"What IS the Creative Industries?"** Open class discussion. What do they think it includes? Collect on the board. Surface assumptions and gaps — don't correct yet, let the conversation run. Keep it lively and challenge any narrow thinking gently. |
| 55–70 | **Folio introduction.** Explain what a folio is and why they're building one. Everything produced from this point is potential evidence. Set up folio folders (physical or digital). Model what a completed and well-organised folio entry looks like. |
| 70–100 | **Employability Self-Evaluation #1 (NAB O3 Review 1).** Learners complete the self-evaluation form honestly — this is their baseline. Emphasise there is no right or wrong at this stage; it is purely about knowing where they are starting from. Enough time here for learners to read each skill carefully and think, not rush. Teacher circulates and countersigns. |

**Evidence generated:** Self-Eval #1 → NAB O3 Review 1 of 3
**NAB criteria met:** O3 — first review completed, signed, countersigned
**Assessor action:** Countersign all self-eval forms before end of session. Note baseline observations for future comparison.
**Differentiation:** Learners who struggle with written self-eval can complete it verbally with teacher scribing, or use a simplified rating scale version.

## WEEK 2 — 18–22 Aug 2025

---

### Tue 19 Aug — Session 2: Marketplace Activity — Exploring the Sectors
**Duration:** 100 minutes
**Learning intention:** Explore the range of sectors within the Creative Industries through independent, self-directed research.

| Time | Activity |
|------|----------|
| 0–5 | **Setup and briefing.** QR code sector stations positioned around the room. Each station covers a different Creative Industries sector. Learners given a recording sheet with prompts: what does this sector do, who works in it, one thing that surprised you. Explain the activity clearly — this is their research, not a worksheet to fill in as fast as possible. |
| 5–55 | **Marketplace rotation.** Learners move freely around stations at their own pace, scanning QR codes, reading info and completing their recording sheet. The longer time here is intentional — encourage depth over speed. Teacher circulates, prompting and challenging: "Tell me something interesting about that sector." "What surprised you there?" "Why do you think that sector is bigger than people expect?" |
| 55–75 | **Class debrief.** Structured share-out — each learner names one sector they hadn't previously thought of as part of the Creative Industries, and one thing that surprised them. Build energy and curiosity. Teacher draws out connections between sectors. |
| 75–100 | **Individual reflection + folio entry.** Learners write a short paragraph in their folio: which sector interests them most, why, and what they still want to find out. Recording sheet goes into folio as evidence. This seeds the sector choice conversations in weeks 3 and 4. |

**Evidence generated:** Marketplace recording sheet + folio reflection → NAB O1 PC(a)
**NAB criteria met:** O1 PC(a) — range of Creative Industries sectors identified and explored
**Note:** Both the recording sheet and the written reflection go into the folio, dated and labelled.

---

### Wed 20 Aug — Session 3: Structure, Scale & Sectors Deep Dive
**Duration:** 100 minutes
**Learning intention:** Understand the structure, scale and economic significance of the Creative Industries. Understand how sectors connect and overlap.

| Time | Activity |
|------|----------|
| 0–10 | **Starter.** "Which sector grabbed you most in the marketplace and why?" Quick pair discussion then share out. Teacher picks up interesting choices and challenges assumptions gently. |
| 10–35 | **Teacher-led input: Structure and scale.** The Creative Industries as an economic sector — contribution to GDP, workforce size, how sectors interconnect. Use a real current example: e.g. a major film production draws simultaneously on music, graphic design, drama, technical theatre, media. Keep it relevant — use examples learners will recognise. |
| 35–65 | **Sector card sort (pairs).** Pairs receive a set of job title cards and sort them into sectors. Include deliberately tricky ones — e.g. music supervisor for a Netflix series (Music or Media?), graphic novel illustrator (Art & Design or Media?), stage manager (Drama or Technical Theatre?). Pairs must agree and be able to defend their choices. Each pair presents their most argued-over card to the class and explains their reasoning. |
| 65–80 | **Sectors overlap discussion.** Whole class — what does the card sort tell us about how the Creative Industries actually works? Is a clean sector boundary realistic? What does that mean for employment and skills? |
| 80–100 | **Sector shortlist + preview of legal/H&S.** Learners narrow their sector interest to two or three options — written in folio, not a final decision yet. Teacher gives a brief heads-up on what's coming in the next session: legal parameters and H&S. Ask learners to think of one legal issue and one safety issue they'd expect in their shortlisted sectors before they arrive on Friday. |

**Evidence generated:** Card sort (observation); sector shortlist (folio)
**NAB criteria met:** O1 PC(a) — deepened understanding of sectors and how they interconnect

### Fri 22 Aug — Session 4: Legal Parameters, Health & Safety + Agencies
**Duration:** 100 minutes
**Learning intention:** Identify and describe legal parameters, H&S legislation and supporting organisations relevant to a Creative Industries sector.

| Time | Activity |
|------|----------|
| 0–10 | **Starter: True or False?** Legal and H&S statements on screen — learners vote and explain their reasoning. E.g. "A photographer automatically owns copyright in every photo they take" (T) · "Health and Safety law doesn't apply to self-employed workers" (F) · "A band can cover any song they like in a live performance without permission" (complicated — generates discussion). |
| 10–30 | **Mini-teach: Legal parameters.** Copyright, intellectual property, contracts, licensing, GDPR basics. Kept sector-relevant throughout — music copyright, photography licensing, stage rights, image rights in film, design IP. Learners note key points in their own words as you go. |
| 30–50 | **Mini-teach: Health & Safety.** COSHH, manual handling, lone working, noise regulations, risk assessment. Sector-specific examples: studio safety, stage rigging, location filming, working at height in technical theatre. Learners note key points. |
| 50–70 | **Mini-teach: Agencies and organisations.** Creative Scotland, Screen Scotland, Equity, Musicians' Union, BECTU, Design Council — who they are, what they do, who they support. Brief input then learners use devices to research two organisations specifically relevant to their shortlisted sector. |
| 70–100 | **Individual written evidence (NAB O1 PCs b, c, d).** Learners write up in their folio: (1) legal parameters for their chosen sector with enough detail to show understanding, (2) H&S legislation with sector-specific relevance, (3) two agencies/organisations with a clear description of what each does. Anything unfinished is completed at the start of Tuesday's session. |

**Evidence generated:** Written legal, H&S and agencies notes → NAB O1 PC(b)(c)(d)
**NAB criteria met:** O1 PC(b) — legal parameters; O1 PC(c) — H&S legislation; O1 PC(d) — agencies
**Assessor action:** Check written responses meet the required depth before signing off — a single sentence per point is not sufficient.

## WEEK 3 — 25–29 Aug 2025

### Tue 26 Aug — Session 5: O1 Completion + Sector Confirmed + O2 Launch
**Duration:** 100 minutes
**Learning intention:** Complete and sign off all Outcome 1 evidence. Confirm chosen sector. Launch Outcome 2 investigation.

| Time | Activity |
|------|----------|
| 0–20 | **O1 evidence catch-up and peer review.** Any learners who didn't finish their legal/H&S/agencies write-up complete it now. Learners who are finished peer-review a partner's work against the NAB criteria — is there enough detail? Is the H&S sector-specific? Are both agencies described clearly? Written peer comment added. |
| 20–40 | **Sector choice conversations.** Teacher moves around the room for one-to-one or small group conversations. Each learner confirms their chosen sector. Prompt questions: Is this where your interests genuinely lie? Have you considered the employment opportunities in this sector? What do you want to do within it eventually? Good relationship-building time — take notes on each learner's choice. |
| 40–60 | **O2 task introduction.** Walk through what Outcome 2 requires in full: four employment opportunities with two freelance issues, three job roles with career paths, one focus job role in depth, and presenting findings. Clarify format options: written report, presentation, blog, podcast, audio recording. Learners begin planning their approach. |
| 60–80 | **Employability skills in the real world.** Show a real job ad from a Creative Industries role. Walk through it together — what do they mean by "proficient in Adobe Creative Suite"? What does "strong interpersonal skills" actually look like in this sector day to day? Discuss: which of these skills do you already have? |
| 80–100 | **Begin O2 employment research.** Learners start researching employment opportunities in their confirmed sector using a structured template. At least one employment opportunity documented before the end of the session. |

**Evidence generated:** O1 all PCs completed and peer-reviewed; O2 PC(a) research started
**NAB criteria met:** O1 — full completion; O2 PC(a) begins

### Wed 27 Aug — Session 6: Employment Opportunities + Freelance Issues (O2 PC a)
**Duration:** 100 minutes
**Learning intention:** Fully investigate and document employment opportunities and freelance/self-employed issues within chosen sector.

| Time | Activity |
|------|----------|
| 0–10 | **Starter: The freelance reality.** Quick poll — do learners think most people in their sector are employed or freelance? Reveal the reality. Why does this matter? What does it change about how you approach your career? Short discussion. |
| 10–70 | **Research: Employment opportunities (NAB O2 PC a).** Learners research and document at least four employment opportunities in their chosen sector. Structured template: job title, what it involves day to day, employed/freelance/both, rough salary or day rate range. Teacher circulates continuously — pushing for specificity, challenging vague descriptions, asking follow-up questions. Learners who finish early add a fifth or sixth opportunity for stronger evidence. |
| 70–90 | **Freelance and self-employed issues (NAB O2 PC a).** Learners identify and describe two significant issues facing freelance or self-employed workers in their sector. These must be sector-specific — e.g. a freelance sound engineer dealing with irregular bookings, no sick pay, own equipment costs — not generic statements. |
| 90–100 | **Share out.** Each learner names one employment opportunity they found that genuinely surprised them. Quick round the room. Builds awareness of the breadth of each sector. |

**Evidence generated:** Four employment opportunities + two freelance issues → NAB O2 PC(a)
**NAB criteria met:** O2 PC(a) — complete

---

### Fri 29 Aug — Session 7: Job Roles, Career Paths + Self-Eval #2
**Duration:** 100 minutes
**Learning intention:** Research and document three job roles with career paths. Complete second employability self-evaluation with peer feedback.

| Time | Activity |
|------|----------|
| 0–10 | **Career path mapping — example.** Show a mapped career path on screen (e.g. runner → floor manager → assistant director → director, or stage crew → deputy stage manager → stage manager → production manager). Discuss: how are careers in the Creative Industries rarely linear? What role do networking, persistence and additional skills play? |
| 10–60 | **Research: Three job roles + career paths (NAB O2 PC b).** Learners research and document three job roles in their chosen sector. For each: a description of what the role involves day to day, and a realistic career path from entry level to a senior position. Teacher pushes for specificity — not just "you could become more senior" but the actual named roles in sequence. Template provided. |
| 60–75 | **Peer check.** Learners swap their job roles and career paths with a partner from the same or a related sector if possible. Partner checks: does this career path make sense? Is the progression realistic? Is there enough detail in each role description? Brief written feedback given — one strength and one suggestion. |
| 75–100 | **Employability Self-Evaluation #2 (NAB O3 Review 2).** Learners complete the second self-eval form, reflecting on how their skills have developed since Review 1 and identifying updated targets. On this occasion peer feedback is required — a classmate responds to at least one employability skills area in writing. Both learner and peer sign. Teacher countersigns before the end of the session. |

**Evidence generated:** Three job roles + career paths → NAB O2 PC(b); Self-Eval #2 with peer feedback → NAB O3 Review 2 of 3
**NAB criteria met:** O2 PC(b) — complete; O3 Review 2 — complete
**Assessor action:** Countersign Self-Eval #2. Confirm peer feedback is present and signed.

## WEEK 4 — 1–5 Sep 2025

### Tue 2 Sep — Session 8: Focus Job Role in Depth (O2 PC c)
**Duration:** 100 minutes
**Learning intention:** Produce a detailed, high-quality profile of one chosen job role.

| Time | Activity |
|------|----------|
| 0–15 | **Starter: Reading a real job spec critically.** Show a detailed real job spec for a role in a Creative Industries sector. Walk through it together — what does "proficient in Adobe Creative Suite" actually mean? What does "strong interpersonal skills" look like in practice in this role? What qualifications are listed as essential vs desirable? Model how to read a job spec with real critical engagement. |
| 15–75 | **Focus job role profile (NAB O2 PC c).** Learners select one of their three researched job roles and produce a detailed written profile covering: (1) the specific employability skills required for this role, (2) key responsibilities described in meaningful detail, (3) qualifications required — both formal (degrees, HNC, specific certifications) and informal (portfolio, experience, networks). Quality is the priority — a thorough two-page profile is worth more than a thin four-page one. Teacher works individually with learners on depth and specificity. |
| 75–90 | **Presentation format planning.** Learners finalise their decision on how they will present their full O2 findings (PC d): written report, presentation, blog post, podcast, audio recording. Brief written plan — format chosen, structure outlined, realistic completion timeline. |
| 90–100 | **Checkpoint.** Learners share their chosen job role and one thing that makes it interesting to them personally. Quick round the room. |

**Evidence generated:** Focus job role profile → NAB O2 PC(c)
**NAB criteria met:** O2 PC(c) — complete

---

### Wed 3 Sep — Session 9: Presenting O2 Findings (O2 PC d)
**Duration:** 100 minutes
**Learning intention:** Organise and present the full O2 investigation findings in a chosen format.

| Time | Activity |
|------|----------|
| 0–10 | **Format examples and quality discussion.** Brief examples of each format option — what makes a blog post effective vs what makes it thin? What does a strong podcast sound like? What does a well-structured presentation look like? Set the quality bar clearly. |
| 10–75 | **Production time — O2 PC(d).** Learners organise and produce their chosen presentation of their full O2 findings. 65 minutes of focused working time. Teacher circulates throughout — checking structure, encouraging depth, helping with format-specific challenges such as recording audio cleanly, structuring a blog with headings, or making a presentation that says something rather than just lists facts. |
| 75–90 | **Peer feedback.** Learners share their work in progress with a partner. Partner gives structured feedback: one specific strength, one specific improvement suggestion. Learners note the feedback in writing and make any adjustments. |
| 90–100 | **Folio entry.** Finished or near-finished presentation added to folio. Learners label it clearly — what it is, which PC it evidences, date. |

**Evidence generated:** Presented O2 findings → NAB O2 PC(d); Outcome 2 complete
**NAB criteria met:** O2 PC(d) — complete; all of Outcome 2 evidenced

---

### Fri 5 Sep — Session 10: Folio Audit + Full O1/O2 Consolidation
**Duration:** 100 minutes
**Learning intention:** Confirm all O1 and O2 evidence is present, complete and of sufficient quality. Address any gaps.

| Time | Activity |
|------|----------|
| 0–20 | **Folio audit.** Learners work through a printed checklist against every O1 and O2 performance criterion. For each: evidence present? Enough detail? Signed and dated? Traffic light it — green (done and good), amber (present but needs improving), red (missing). |
| 20–65 | **Gap fill and improvement time.** Learners work on amber and red items. Teacher prioritises who needs most support and works with individuals. Learners with everything green work on an extension task: write an evaluative paragraph reflecting on what they have learned about the Creative Industries that genuinely surprised them, and what it means for their own future in the sector. |
| 65–85 | **Consolidation discussion.** Whole class. What has surprised them most across the unit? What job role would they actually want? What sector feels most real and relevant to them now? Useful diagnostic info for how to pitch U2 — listen carefully to what comes up. |
| 85–100 | **NAB preview.** Walk through how the NAB window works — open-book, they use their folio and notes, it is about demonstrating understanding not reproducing memorised facts. Remove anxiety. Run through what the assessor will look for on each PC. Answer questions. |

**Evidence generated:** All outstanding O1 and O2 evidence completed; folio organised and checked
**NAB criteria met:** Final sign-off on all O1 and O2 PCs
