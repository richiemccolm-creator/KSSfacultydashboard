WARM UP· Slow Motion Walk 0–8 min
0–8
minutes
Slow Motion Walk
1
Walk the space in extreme slow motion — every single movement must be deliberate and smooth.
2
Your teacher will call tempo changes. Respond immediately — from very slow to a complete freeze.
3
Think about the S in S.P.E.C.S. — Slow. This is the element we are drilling right now.
4
Keep your whole body engaged — arms, face, posture. Even slowed down, your body should tell a story.
THINK ABOUT THIS AS YOU MOVE
Why does slowing down make mime more readable — and more believable — for the audience?
Slowing down is not the same as being boring. Slowing down gives the audience time to believe you.

ACTIVITY· Mime a Task — Choose one and perform it 22–38 min
22–38
minutes
Mime a Task
🍞 Making Toast
Get the bread. Put it in. Wait. It pops. Butter it. Eat it. Every object must feel real.
🎣 Fishing
Cast the line. Wait patiently. Feel a tug. Fight to reel it in. React to what you catch.
🎁 Wrapping a Gift
The paper, the tape, the ribbon. It keeps going wrong. Show your growing frustration.
🪴 Repotting a Plant
The soil, the roots, the new pot. Something goes wrong halfway through. How do you fix it?
🔧 Fixing a Bike
The tools, the chain, the frustration. You think you've fixed it. You haven't.
🍰 Decorating a Cake
Careful precision. Icing, decorations, a candle. Then it all slides off one side.
REMEMBER: 3 clear steps minimum Show the weight & texture Define the space around you Slow every transition down

ACTIVITY· Watching Others — Use this checklist 22–38 min
Observer Checklist
While your group performs, use this checklist. Give one piece of specific feedback afterwards.
Was each action Simple enough to read in one glance?
Were the movements Precise — deliberate and controlled, with no drifting?
Was the physicality Exaggerated enough to be visible from the back of the room?
Was every action Clear — could you always tell exactly what the object was?
Were the movements Slow enough — especially the transitions between steps?
YOUR FEEDBACK MUST BE SPECIFIC
Don't say "it was good." Say which S.P.E.C.S. element was strongest and exactly why you could tell.

EVALUATE· Write two sentences evaluating a partner's mime 46–50 min
46–50

Written Evaluation
Write two sentences evaluating your partner's mime. Use at least two S.P.E.C.S. elements and two Drama vocabulary words.
"Their mime was [S.P.E.C.S. element] because…"
Name a specific moment. Explain what you saw in their body, gesture or expression that proved it.
"One S.P.E.C.S. element they could develop further is… because…"
Be constructive and specific — explain what it would look like if that element were stronger.
EXAMPLE — THIS IS WHAT A STRONG EVALUATION LOOKS LIKE
"Their mime was precise and slow — you could clearly see the weight of the toast because they exaggerated the effort in their posture and slowed down every movement. One element they could develop further is clear — at one point I wasn't sure whether the object was a knife or a spatula, which broke the believability."
