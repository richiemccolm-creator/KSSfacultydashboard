S.P.E.C.S. Feedback Frame
Give feedback using these sentence starters. You must use at least two S.P.E.C.S. elements and two Drama vocabulary words.
"Their mime was [S.P.E.C.S. element] because…"
Name a specific moment. Describe exactly what you saw in their body — gesture, posture, levels, eye contact.
"The moment that communicated meaning most clearly was… because…"
Point to one specific moment in the performance. Why did it work — which S.P.E.C.S. elements were at their strongest?
"One S.P.E.C.S. element they could develop further is… because…"
Be constructive. Explain what it would look like if that element were improved — not just that it wasn't good enough.
REMEMBER — GOOD FEEDBACK IS SPECIFIC
Don't say "it was good." Say which element, which moment, and exactly why.

28–42
minutes
Introducing Melodrama
MIME
No words — body tells everything
Controlled, precise, deliberate
Believability is the goal
Audience observes in silence
S.P.E.C.S. applied throughout
MELODRAMA
Exaggerated emotion — bigger than real life
Stock characters — instantly recognisable
Audience interaction — boo, cheer, gasp
Clear moral — good triumphs over evil
Exaggerated is taken to the absolute extreme
HOW S.P.E.C.S. CONNECTS MIME AND MELODRAMA
In mime, S.P.E.C.S. makes movement readable. In melodrama, Exaggerated is turned up to maximum — everything is amplified, larger, more dramatic. Clear and Slow become even more important — the audience needs time to react.
DISCUSS
Which S.P.E.C.S. element do you think matters most in melodrama — and why is it different from mime?

Villain & Hero
1
Walk the space as a melodrama villain. Hunched, sneering, slow and deliberate. Wringing hands. Sidelong glances. The audience must want to boo you on sight.
2
When your teacher claps — instantly switch to a melodrama hero. Upright, noble, chin raised, open gestures. The audience must want to cheer.
3
Switch back and forth — notice what changes in your body each time. Compare it to your status work in Lesson 3.
DISCUSS AFTER THE EXERCISE
How is a melodrama villain physically different from a high-status character in mime? What S.P.E.C.S. elements changed — and how?
In melodrama, your body must communicate your character to the audience in the first 5 seconds. There is no time to build — you must be immediately, unmistakably yourself.
